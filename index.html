Task 2. Develop Adaptive Monitoring Plan
A. Work performed on Task 2.

The Adaptive Management Plan for this project should be seen at minimum as a living document, and, as an active, constant process.

An updated version of the Adaptive Management Plan, and Biological Sampling Plan, were completed in June 2020. The elevation and biological sampling plans continue to evolve as we have now completed the second winter season of postconstruction oyster sampling, and ongoing water quality sampling. As an example, the variability in oyster counts observed in each strata (substrate, harvest status, location) collected in each winter sampling period is incorporated into our estimate of numbers samples required to achieve a known power of detecting change of particular magnitude for each upcoming sampling period. This sampling effort is then updated mid-winter sampling season based on observed count data collected during the first half of the winter sampling. Details of this adaptive approach are included in a recent peer-reviewed publication that was published this quarter in open-access journal PeerJ.

Moore JF, Pine III WE. 2021. Bootstrap methods can help evaluate monitoring program performance to inform restoration as part of an adaptive management program. PeerJ 9:e11378 https://doi.org/10.7717/peerj.11378

  1. Progress made towards Task 2 milestones.

Task 5. Post-Construction Monitoring
Work performed on Task 5.

A. We have continued the water quality sampling program, collecting continuous information and downloading sondes every two weeks (see Sections 2 - 5). We have a relational database to track these data and to develop relationships between water quality data, survey information, and oyster populations (Task 7). Water quality information from this project is publicly available and summarized on the website https://lcroysterproject.github.io/oysterproject/. We employed a group of eight commercial oyster harvesters from the Suwannee Sound region to assist with an experimental assessment of sub-tidal oyster populations. Data collected from this effort in June 2021 are still being assessed.

B. Progress made towards Task 5 milestones. . Postconstruction biological sampling of oyster density and size on all study reefs completed for each winter sampling period. Data entered, summarized, and analyses complete (updates provided in last quarterly report). Sampling effort for winter 2021-2022 currently being planned.

. Postconstruction elevation sampling was completed by GF Young Surveyors in winter/spring 2020 - 2021 and we are awaiting data from this sub-contract.

. Work with commercial oyster harvesters to experimentally assess sub-tidal oyster populations near Lone Cabbage and at a control site.

Subtask 6.2. Post-construction education and outreach A paper brochure about the project continues to be distributed to the public via the local hardware and tackle shops, marinas, the Cedar Key Chamber of Commerce, and UF/IFAS Nature Coast Biological Station during the reporting period. We maintain two public educational displays about the Lone Cabbage restoration project at high traffic boat ramps in Cedar Key and Suwannee. A third display at a remote boat ramp in Dixie County (Shired Island) was badly vandalized, and we removed the display.

We continue to update public information about the project primarily through the various electronic and social media produced by the Nature Coast Biological Station in Cedar Key https://ncbs.ifas.ufl.edu/. Data can be seen by the public for water quality and other related material via the project Shiny Ap of data visualization available here https://lcroysterproject.github.io/oysterproject/

We published a paper in the open-access peer-reviewed journal “PeerJ” on monitoring program performance related to an adaptive management program (see Task 2A). This paper includes supplemental material that can be used to replicate the simulations used in the paper in other situations to inform monitoring program performance. Publication costs were not charged to the project for this manuscript. As an open-access publication the information is available to the general public and resource practitioners conducting similar research.

We met in-person with FWC staff to review ongoing and proposed oyster restoration efforts in the Suwannee Sound region. We continue to share with FWC internal documents related to Lone Cabbage Reef monitoring program design, data management, automation tools for summarizing data, water quality monitoring array design, and related content. Following discussions with NFWF staff, we anticipate having a meeting with FWC, NFWF, and UF personnel in fall 2021 to discuss sampling planning efforts for NFWF funded projects in the region.
